Introducing ExploreLocal: My First Figma Project and UX Design

Your Ultimate Event Finder App

As I embarked on my journey to become a front-end developer, I took a transformative leap by completing the renowned Google UX Design course on Coursera. As part of this immersive training, I undertook a hands-on project using Figma. In this project, I created an intuitive app with a clear purpose: to empower individuals in their quest for nearby and upcoming events. The idea was that users can effortlessly explore a vibrant array of cultural experiences right in their vicinity. Gone are the days of navigating external websites; this app allows users to conveniently search for events and book their tickets directly within the platform.

Crafted with a user-centric approach, I employed UX design principles to ensure an engaging and seamless experience. This project showcases my ability to transform ideas into tangible, user-centered interfaces and demonstrates my commitment to continuously honing my front-end development skills.

Subpage Design: Focused Recipe Presentation with Added Features

CThe subpage dedicated to presenting recipes aimed to prioritize the recipe itself as the central focus. In line with the overall design consistency, the layout incorporated additional elements to enhance user experience and engagement. To maintain consistency and ease of navigation, the subpage retained the prominent search function situated at the top. This ensured that users could effortlessly search for specific recipes, even when exploring individual recipe pages. On the right-hand side of the subpage, a showcase of the most popular recipes was thoughtfully included. This feature provided users with easy access to highly regarded recipes, promoting exploration and discovery. By strategically placing the showcase, users were encouraged to explore popular recipes without diverting attention from the main recipe content. Furthermore, a dedicated comment box was integrated into the subpage. This addition aimed to foster user interaction and enable visitors to share their thoughts, feedback, and culinary experiences related to the recipe. The comment box created a sense of community and engagement, enabling users to actively participate and contribute to the blog's growing conversation. The subpage design struck a balance between highlighting the recipe itself as the main focal point while incorporating valuable features. By seamlessly integrating the search function, showcasing popular recipes, and providing a comment box, the redesigned subpage aimed to enhance user experience, encourage exploration, and facilitate user engagement within the culinary blog. Overall, this project exemplified the successful incorporation of essential elements while maintaining a user-centered approach. The subpage design emphasized the recipe's significance, while carefully integrating complementary features to enrich the overall user experience and promote active user participation.

User Research and Iterative Testing: Streamlining the Culinary Blog Layout

During the research phase of the project, a carefully selected audience consisting of 10 individuals participated in prototype testing. This diverse group included one participant from the 60+ age group and five participants ranging from ages 20 to 40. The findings from the initial testing phase revealed that the blog's layout felt overwhelming due to the presence of numerous features, leading to confusion among users. Based on these valuable insights, design decisions were made to refine the layout and improve clarity. The first step involved eliminating the carousel feature to reduce visual complexity and enhance the user's focus on essential content. Additionally, more spacing was introduced between different sections to provide a sense of breathing room, reducing visual clutter and improving overall legibility. To further address the feedback, an emphasis was placed on enhancing the visual hierarchy within the layout. This involved carefully assigning visual weight to different elements, ensuring that important information and navigational cues were clear and easily distinguishable. Subsequently, another round of testing was conducted to validate the design changes. The results of this iterative testing phase demonstrated improved user satisfaction and comprehension. Participants found the updated layout to be more inviting, less overwhelming, and easier to navigate. The research and iterative testing process played a pivotal role in optimizing the culinary blog's layout. By actively incorporating user feedback, the design team successfully simplified the layout, introduced adequate spacing, and established a clear visual hierarchy. These improvements aimed to create a more user-friendly experience, ensuring that visitors of all age groups could comfortably explore the blog's content.

Consistent Category Filtered Page: Seamlessly Navigating Culinary Categories

To maintain consistency throughout the website, the category filtered page shares a similar design to the homepage. However, the key distinction lies in its ability to showcase recipes filtered by the specific category chosen by the user. At the top of the page, users are provided with clear information about the category they are currently browsing. This ensures they have a clear understanding of their location within the culinary categories. Directly below, an intuitive navigation feature allows users to easily switch between different categories, enabling seamless exploration and access to diverse culinary content. This user-friendly navigation empowers visitors to effortlessly discover recipes that align with their specific interests. To enhance user engagement and showcase popular recipes, a dedicated section featuring the most popular recipes is positioned at the bottom of the category filtered page. This valuable feature, as requested by the client, enables users to explore highly regarded recipes and discover culinary gems within the chosen category. By incorporating consistent design elements from the homepage, such as color schemes, typography, and overall layout, the category filtered page ensures a cohesive user experience throughout the website. This consistency promotes ease of use and familiarity for users as they navigate between different culinary categories. The inclusion of category-specific information, intuitive navigation options, and a showcase of popular recipes on the category filtered page reflects a user-centered approach. It aims to facilitate seamless exploration, encourage engagement, and provide visitors with an enjoyable culinary journey through the blog's diverse categories.
